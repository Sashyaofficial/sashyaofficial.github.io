Anyang - The Cradle of Ancient China

Anyang was one of the ancient capitals of the Shang dynasty. It holds a key place in Chinese archaeology due to the rich burial sites and oracle bone inscriptions found there.

Artifacts like jade carvings, bronze vessels, and oracle bones provide invaluable insight into early Chinese civilization, religion, and governance.

The Bodhisattvas - Guardians of Wisdom

Bodhisattvas are revered figures in Buddhist traditions, embodying compassion and enlightenment. They are central to the teachings of Mahayana Buddhism.

The figure of Avalokiteshvara, for example, represents infinite compassion. Through symbolic art, such as sculptures and paintings, Bodhisattvas help guide others to spiritual liberation.

Cultural Insights from the Ancient East

The East has long been home to profound philosophical, spiritual, and artistic traditions. Learn more about how ideas from ancient China, India, and beyond shaped the cultures we know today.

Confucianism and Daoism

Confucianism emphasizes morality, family loyalty, and social harmony, while Daoism encourages living in harmony with the Dao, or "the way." These philosophies have profoundly influenced East Asian societies for centuries.

The Spread of Buddhism

Buddhism, originating in India, spread to China, Korea, and Japan. Its teachings of compassion, non-violence, and mindfulness have shaped spiritual practices and artistic expression throughout the East.

Art and Architecture

East Asian art, particularly in China and Japan, is rich in symbolism. Architectural wonders, such as the Great Wall of China and the Buddhist cave temples of Dunhuang, are testaments to the advanced cultures that produced them.
